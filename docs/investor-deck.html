Why the app was built

Potholes are a high-frequency, high-friction civic problem. Citizens need a fast way to report them. Administrators need structured, trustworthy, non-duplicate inputs they can actually process.

1
Civic responsibility needs a practical channelResidents often see issues but lack a reliable, visible mechanism to escalate them with confidence.
2
Operational systems break on poor-quality reportsBlurry images, weak location data, duplicate submissions, and unclear severity slow response and reduce trust.
3
Closing the loop mattersReporting becomes habit-forming only when citizens see progress, acknowledgment, and resolution signals after submission.
4
Gamification makes civic action repeatablePoints, badges, and leaderboards convert one-time reporting into repeat participation, while preserving a public-good frame.

What Mana Rasta solves

It transforms a vague civic complaint into a structured workflow: guided capture, validated location, severity tagging, operational review, analytics, and citizen-visible follow-through.

Citizen Value
  • Fast pothole reporting in under a minute
  • Clear status visibility after submission
  • Recognition for responsible participation
  • Simple, mobile-first experience
Operational Value
  • Better report quality at intake
  • Lower duplicate and fraud noise
  • Structured admin workflow and SLA tracking
  • Actionable ward and zone analytics

Civic responsibility with incentives

The platform treats citizens as active participants in city upkeep. Gamification is used as an incentive layer, not as a gimmick: it rewards valid reporting behavior while reinforcing public contribution.

Machine learning narrows the problem

The project includes a dedicated ML service focused on capture quality and pothole-focused analysis. Its role is not to replace operations. Its role is to reduce noise before reports enter the system.

A
Quality gatingBlurry, low-light, cropped, or glare-heavy images can be flagged before they degrade the workflow.
B
Pothole-focused detectionCandidate detection helps separate likely pothole images from non-actionable surfaces and edge cases.
C
Guidance loopInstead of silently failing, the system can instruct the user to hold steady, move closer, or reframe the subject.
D
Operational efficiencyBetter quality at intake means fewer wasted reviews, lower duplicate noise, and cleaner downstream analytics.

Fraud prevention and abuse control

  • Rate limiting on backend routes to control spam and OTP abuse
  • Fraud service to score suspicious usage patterns
  • Duplicate image checks to reduce repeated submissions
  • Moderation service to block unsafe or invalid image content
  • Capture logs and analytics to trace suspicious behavior over time

Future scope

Mana Rasta is designed as a pothole-first platform, but the same workflow model can support adjacent civic issues.

Broken streetlights

Image-based issue capture, location tagging, assignment, and status visibility.

Footpath issues

Encroachment, breakage, accessibility issues, and surface damage reporting.

Waterlogging

Severity and geo-aware reporting for rainfall and drainage-related incidents.

Other urban defects

Open category expansion using the same citizen, admin, analytics, and abuse-control foundations.
